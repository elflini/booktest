Survival Analysis

Chapter 1 Survival Analysis?

1.1 Introduction

  • 생존분석(survival analysis)은 생존시간(survival time) 자료를 분석하는 통계적 방법

  • 넓은 의미에서 생존시간이란 어떤 정의된 시점부터 특정한 사건(event)이 관측될 때까지의 시간을 의미: time to the occurrence of an event

  • 보통 관심이 있는 사망이나 질병의 발생, 재발 또는 좋지 않은 경험을 사건으로 정의하는 경우가 많기 때문에 사건의 발생을 ‘실패’(failure)라 하고 생존시간을 실패시간(failure time) 이라고도 함

  • Examples of failures:

    • 환자가 수술시부터 사망할 때까지의 시간(occurrence of death)
    • 처치 후 재발할 때까지의 시간(recurrence of a disease)
    • 어떤 처리를 받은 후 특정 반응을 나타낼 때까지 걸린 시간(disappearance of a symptom)

  • Survival data: survival data contain three types of information

    • 생존시간(survival times)
    • 환자 특성(patient characteristics)
    • 처리 할당(treatment assignments)

  • Example) Data for a subset of 30 resected Melanoma patients

  • Information about survival times: -Time until death (survival time) -Remission duration (event free survival time or disease free survival time)

  • Information about patient characteristics:

    • Age in years,
    • Sex (1=Male; 2=Female),
    • Disease stage (2, 3A, 3B, 4A).

  • Information about treatment assignment:

    • Treatment (1=BCG; 2=C.parvum)

  • Information about censoring status:

    • Censoring status (+ if the recorded time is shorter than the survival time)

  • Frequently asked questions in Survival Analyses

  • Median survival time:

    • What is the median remission time under a particular treatment? That is, what is the shortest remission time for 50% of patients?
    • Exactly half of the population survives units of time or longer.

  • Mean Survival time:

    • What is the mean survival time under a particular treatment?
    • That is, on the average, how long a patient is expected to live under a given treatment?

  • Survival probability:

    • What is the probability that a patient on a given treatment will live(survive) at least 5 years

  • Comparison of survival times:

    • Is there a difference between the disease free survival times of patients under the two conditions?
    • Is one treatment better than the other?
    • Which treatment is better?

  • Prognostic factors:

    • Do age, gender, and disease stage affect the way the disease progresses over time?
    • That is, are age, sex, and disease stage useful for predicting the course of the disease?

  • Homogeneity of treatment groups:

    • Are the two treatment groups homogeneous w.r.t age, sex, disease?

  • Risk factors:

    • What are the primary risk factors associated with a given health related events?
    • That is, what factors increase the likelihood of disease? Is smoking a risk factor for lung cancer?

  • Definition of end point

    • Death from any cause or from a specific cause is the end-point for the analysis of mortality in clinical and epidemiological studies.
    • In clinical studies, researchers also interested in evaluating the ability of treatment to prevent or to delay recurrent disease as well as death.
    • Consequently, the end-point is recurrence or death whichever occurs first.
    • Each patient will have a failure time or, if none of the events occurred, a time at which he is alive and event free.
    • It is called as event free survival or disease free survival.
    • For overall survival it is intended that the end-point of the analysis is death from any cause.

Primary end point

  1. OS: Overall survival / Death
  2. PFS: Progression free survival / progression + death
  3. TTP: Time to disease progression / progression
  4. TTD: Time to treatment discontinuation / treatment stop
  5. PPs: Post progression survival / time from progression to death
  6. PFS2: 2nd PFS / time after progression to 2nd progression

Goal of survival analysis

  1. 생존데이터로부터 생존함수(survival function)와 위험함수(hazard function)를 추정

  1. 생존함수와 위험함수의 비교

  1. 설명변수들과 생존시간의 관계 측정
  • e.g) Regression model
    • Accelerated failure time model (AFT)- parametric model
      • \(\epsilon \sim\) {exponential, Wibull, Log-normal}
    • Cox proportional hazards model - semi parametric

\[ \lambda(t, \mathbf{x})=\lambda_0(t)e^{\beta_0+\beta_1 x_1 +\cdots +\beta_p x_p} \]

1.2 Basic Concept of Study Design

  • Survival analysis: Deals with analysis of data that have “time to an event”
  • Survival time: Time until an event occurs
  • Event
    • relapse of a disease
    • death
    • response to a treatment
    • to get a job for the unemployed
    • to learn a skill for the children

1.2.1 Study designs

  • Retrospective studies
  • Prospective studies

1) Retrospective study

  • Gather past data from selected cases and controls
  • Case-control studies
    • Cases of a specific design are ascertained from population-based disease registry or lists of hospital admissions
    • Controls are sampled either as disease-free individuals from the population at risk or as hospitalized patiensts having a diagnosis other than the one under study
  • Advantages of retrospective design
    • Economical to obtain answers quickly because the cases are already availabel
  • Disadvantages of retrospective design
    • Limitation in the accuracy of exposure history and uncertainty about the appropriateness of the control sample
  • Example

      Note: - In this example, conditional distribution given row is nonsense, but conditional distribution given column makes sense.

  • Difference of the proportions: conditional distribution of oral contraceptive, given myocardial infarction

\[ \pi_{1|1} - \pi_{1|2} \]

2) Prospective study

  • Sample subjects from population
    • After 20 years we observe the rates of myocardial infarction
  • Using this example

    • Cohort studies: woman make their own choices about whether to use oral contraceptive and we simply observe in the future who suffers myocardial infarction

    • Clinical trial: we randomly allocate subjects to groups who will use or not use oral contraceptive

    • Cross-sectional studies: samples woman and classifies them simultaneously on both variables

3) Summary

  • Prospective study (clinical trials, cohort studies)
    • Exposed and unexposed groups are identified and followed over time to compare incidence of disease
  • Retrospective study (case-control study)
    • Diseased and disease-free subjects are identified and their exposure history investigated
  • Cross-sectional study
    • Sample of \(n\) subjects of unknown disese and exposure status is identified and disease status and prior exposure status are assessed simultaneously

  • In a cross-sectional design, we fix \(n\), and then measure \(n_{11}\), \(n_{12}\), \(n_{21}\), \(n{22}\), from which we form \(p_{ij}=n_{ij}/n, \,\,\, i,j=1,2\), which are appropriate estimators of \(\pi_{ij}, \,\,\, i,j=1,2\)

  • 연구 디자인간 특성 비교

  • cf. Cohort study
    • One enrolls a group of persons and follows them over a certain period to observe the time at which a disease or another end point develops

1.2.2 Clinical trials

  • A prospective study comparing the effect and value of interventions(s) against control in human subjects

  • Prospective not retrospective

  • Intervention / equipment

    • Preventive drug
    • Therapeutic device
    • Diagnostic procedure
    • Biologic

  • Control group

    • No intervention
    • Placebo
    • Current standard therapy
    • Previous standard

  • Human are not animals

    • Ethics (윤리성)
    • Informed consent (사전동의)

Definition of clinical trials

A clinical trial is an experiment on humans for the purpose of evaluating one or more potentially beneficial therapies where the investigator has control of some features of the trial

- Essential point - Use results based on a limited sample to make inference to the general population with a given medical condition

  • Single & double blind clinical trial
    • Single blind: A clinical trial where the patient does not know the identify of the treatment received
    • Double blind: A clinical trial in which neither the patient nor the treating physician knows the identity of the treatment being administered

1.2.2.1 Phase I

  • New treatment (usually drugs) is being tried on humans for the first time

  • Aim is to fine acceptable range of dosages and schedule

  • Seeking maximum tolerated dose (MTD)

  • 3 methods in Phase I

    • Standard Method (SM)
    • Continual reassessment method (CRM)
    • Accelerated titration desing (ATD)

  • Basic principles

    • For Phase I clinical trials, utilize the results from animal studies to figure out potential toxicity
    • Healthy applications are involved
    • Slow dose escalation

1.2.2.2 Phase II

  • Treatment is to be given to humans to determine if it has any beneficial activity
  • Dosage and schedules may not be optimum
  • Estimate of drug activity
  • Decide if drug warrants further testing (Phase III)
  • Estimate of serious toxicities

1.2.2.3 Phase III

  • Comparative trial: to compare experimental or new therapies with standard therapy or competitive therapies
  • Provide efficacy and safety of drug or therapy
  • Various designs
    • No control
    • Historical control
    • Concurrent
    • Randomized
  • Tesing for treatment effect

1.2.2.4 Phase IV

  • Long term post phase III follow-up
  • Concern for safety
  • Post-marketing surveillance


  • A clinical trial consists of two periods

  • Enrollment period (0, \(\pi\))
    • Subjects enter the study sequentially and are randomized to receive either therapeutic agent or placebo (or the standard therapeutic treatment)
  • Follow-up period (\(\pi_1\), \(\pi_2\))
    • Recruited subjects are followed the time of the primary ending event, or to time \(\pi_2\), - eg) 3 years enrollment and 2 years of follow-up (\(\pi_1=3\), \(\pi_2=5\))
      • No patient are enrolled during the last 2 years.

Summary

  • Observational studies
    • Observe who choose each group and who has the outcome of interest
      • Case-control
      • Cohort
      • Cross-sectional studies
  • Experimental design
    • Clinical trial investigator has control over which receive each treatment


1.3 중도절단(Censoring)

  • 생존 데이터가 다른 데이터와 구별되는 이유 중 하나는 중도절단 자료(censored data)를 포함하기 때문

  • 중도절단(censoring)은 생존시간이 불완전하게 관찰되는 경우를 의미함

    • 생존 시간에 대한 어느 정도의 정보는 가지고 있지만 정확한 생존시간을 모를 때 발생
    • 예) 백혈병 환자를 대상으로 관해상태에 들어간 후 재발할 때까지 관찰하는 연구에서 연구 종료 시점까지 환자가 관해상태를 유지한다면 환자의 생존시간은 중도절단된 것
    • 관찰한 것보다 생존시간이 길다는 것은 알지만, 완전한 생존시간을 알 수 없음


중도절단 종류

  1. 우중도절단(right-censored)
    • 연구가 종료될 때까지 이벤트가 발생하지 않았거나 중도에 탈락하여 정확한 생존기간을 알 수 없는 경우
    • 대부분의 생존자료는 우중도절단에 해당함
  2. 좌중도절단(left-censored)
    • Event happened but we don’t know when it began
    • At start time, the event has already occurred
    • 예) HIV에 노출된 후 처음으로 HIV 검사에서 양성으로 나오는 것을 사건으로하는 생존분석의 경우, 바이러스에 노출된 시간을 정확히 알지 못하는 경우
  3. 구간중도절단(interval-censored)
    • 실제 생존시간이 어떤 구간 내에 발생하는 경우
    • 예) HIV 검사를 2번 하였고 시점 t1에서는 음성으로 나왔으며, t2에서는 양성으로 나왔지만 정확한 노출시간을 알지 못하는 경우 구간 (t1, t2) 사이에서 구간중도절단 됨

중도절단 발생 예시1

  • A psychiatrist wanted to know the age at which a certain group of children learned to perform a task in the study sample
  1. Some children already knew the task in the study sample (left censoring)
  2. Some children learded the task during the study (uncensored)
  3. Some children did not learn the task (right censoring)

중도절단 발생 예시2

  • 2번 환자의 경우 유방암으로 인한 사망으로 발생하기까지 4년이 걸렸으며, 원하는 이벤트가 발생했기 때문에 중도절단이 아님

  • 1번 환자의 경우 심근경색으로 사망하기까지 6년이 관찰되었으나, 원하는 사건인 유방암으로 인한 사망이 아니기때문에 실질적으로 유방암으로 인한 사망이 발생할때까지 완전한 시간을 알 수 없음. 따라서 중도절단 자료임

1.3.1 Right censoring

  • The exact value of the survival time is unknown
  • It is known that its value is greater than or equal to some \(C\)

\[ Y_i = min(T_i, C_i) \]

  • The exact survival time becomes incomplete at the right side of the follow-up

Example

  • Suppose all observations start from time 0
  • Observation terminated at a fixed time \(A\)

  • Patient 4: \(T_4 \ge 5 \rightarrow 5^+\), loss to follow-up (e.g. moving)
    • \(C_4=5\), \(Y_4=min(T_4, C_4)\)
  • Patient 5: \(T_5 \ge 9 \rightarrow 9^+\), withdrawal (e.g. side effect)
    • \(C_5=9\), \(Y_5=min(T_5, C_5)\)
  • Patient 3: \(T_3 \ge 10 \rightarrow 10^+\), \(C_3=10\), \(Y_3=min(T_3, C_3)\)

1.3.1.1 Random (right) censoring

  • An individual’s censoring time is random
  • Example for random censoring
    • Loss to follow-up (e.g. 이사, 이민 등)
    • Drop out (e.g. 치료 거부, 부작용 등)
    • Death due to another cause (e.g. 교통사고에 의한 사망)
    • End of study (e.g. 갑작스런 예산삭감으로 인한 연구 종료)

1.3.1.2 Type I censoring

  • An individual’s survival time is observed only if \(T_i \le C_i\), where \(C_i\) is fixed time
  • Assumption: Random censoring
  1. \(C_i\)’s are random variable
  2. \(T_i\) and \(C_i\) are independent
  3. Not necessarily for \(C_i\) to be identically distributed

1.3.1.3 Type II censoring

  • The number of failure observations is decided before the data are collected

  • The data consist of \(r\) smallest observations

\[ T_{(1)} \le T_{(2)} \le \cdots \le T_{(r)} \]

      i.e., observation terminated as soon as \(r\) items fail (\(r \le n\))

  • Order statistic: \(T_{(1)}, T_{(2)}, \cdots, T_{(n)}\)

  • Observation: \(T_{(1)}, T_{(2)}, \cdots , T_{(r)}, T_{(r)}^+, \cdots, T_{(r)}^+\)

  • 예) 100마리의 동물 중 20마리가 사망하면 연구 종료

1.3.2 Left censoring

  • The exact value of the survival time is unknown
  • But, it is known that its value is smaller than or equal to some value \(L\)
  • The exact survival time becomes incomplete at the left side of the follow-up period
    • 예) 병원에 실려온 환자가 도착하기 전에 사망한 경우
    • 어린이를 대상으로 언제 말을 처음으로 시작하는지 조사하는 경우, 조사시점에 이미 말을 하는 경우

Note

  • Observations start at random

1.4 Failure Time Distribution

  • $T $: failure time of an individual from a homogenous population
  • The probability distribution of T can be specified in
    • Survival function
    • Probability density function (pdf)
    • Hazard function

1.4.1 생존함수(survival function)

  • 생존시간 \(T_1, T_2, \ldots, T_n\)은 서로 독립이고 동일한 분포 \(F\)를 갖는 확률변수

  • 중도절단시간 \(C_1, C_2, \ldots, C_n\)은 서로 독립이고 동일한 분포 \(G\)를 갖는 확률변수

  • 생존시간 \(T_1, T_2, \ldots, T_n\)을 모두 관측하지 못하는 대신

\[ X_i = min(T_i, C_i)\] \[\delta_i =I(T_i < C_i ) \]

      로 정의되는 \((T_1, \delta_i), (T_2, \delta_2), \ldots, (T_n , \delta_n )\)을 관측

  • 임의중도절단의 경우 일반적으로 생존시간 \(T_i\)와 중도절단시간 \(C_i\)가 서로 독립 가정

1.4.1.1 생존함수\(S(t)\)

  • 생존시간을 \(T(\ge 0)\)라 하고 \(T\)가 확률밀도함수 \(f(t)\)와 분포함수 \(F(t)\)를 가질 때,

\[S(t)=P(T>t)=1-P(T\le t)=1-F(t), \,\,\, 0<t<\infty\]

  • 생존함수는 환자가 \(t\)시간 이상 생존할 확률
    • 예) \(S(3)=0.42\): \(42\%\) of patients survived beyond 3 years
  • survival curve: the graph of \(S(t)\) vs \(t\)
    • steep curve: shorter survival
    • gradual or flat curve: longer survival
  • Median survival time: \(T_{0.5}\)
    • \(T_{0.5}\) is a time point at which \(S(T_{0.5})=0.5\)
    • Exactly half of population survives \(T_{0.5}\) units of time or longer

  • Population mean \(\mu\): area under the survival curve

  • Height

\[ \begin{aligned} Height &=S(t)-S(t+\delta) \\ &= Pr(T>t) - Pr(T>t+\delta)\\ &= Pr(die\,\,\, within\,\,\, (t, t+\delta)) \end{aligned} \]

  • Width=\(t\)

  • Area for the box

\[ t \times Pr(die\,\,\, within\,\,\, (t, t+\delta)) \]

  • Area under the curve

\[ \begin{aligned} Area &= \sum t \times Pr(die\,\,\, within\,\,\, (t, t+\delta)) \\ &= E(T) \\ &= \mu \,\,\, (as \,\,\, \delta \rightarrow 0) \end{aligned} \]

Other proof

\[ \begin{aligned} \mu=E(T)&=\int_o^{\infty}tf(t)dt=[t\cdot (-)S(t)]_0^{\infty}-\int_0^{\infty}-S(t)dt\\ &= \int_0^{\infty}S(t)dt \end{aligned} \]

      \(\therefore \,\,\, \hat{\mu}=\int_o^{\infty}\hat{S}(t)dt\)

  • Problem
    • If the last observation is censored then \(\hat{S}(t)=0 \rightarrow \hat{\mu}=\infty\)

\[ \hat{\mu}=\int_0^{t_(n)}\hat{S}(t)dt \]

1.4.1.2 생존함수의 이론적 특성

  1. \(S(t)\) is monotone non-increasing

      - 생존함수는 증가하지 않음. 즉 \(t\)가 증가함에 따라 감소함

  1. \(S(0)=1\), \(S(\infty)=\lim_{t\rightarrow \infty}S(t)=0\)

      - \(t=0\)일 때 \(S(t)=S(0)=1\). 즉, 연구가 시작되었을 때 아직 사건이 발생하지 않았으므로 생존 함수값은 1.

      - \(t=\infty\)일 때 \(S(t)=S(\infty)=0\). 즉, 이론적으로는 연구 기간의 제한이 없다면 결국 모든 대상자가 사망하므로 생존함수는 0.

      - 현실적으로 여러가지 제약에 의해 모든 연구 대상자에게 사건이 발생하지 않을 수도 있기 때문에 반드시 연구 종료 시 추정된 생존함수가 0이 되지는 않음

  1. \(S(t)\) is left continuous

\[ \lim_{t\rightarrow t_0^-}S(t)=S(t_0) \leftrightarrow S(t_0^-)=S(t_0), \,\,\, S(t_0^+)\le S(t_0) \]

  1. \(1-survival \,\,\, function\) = cumulative distribute function (CDF)

1.4.2 Continuous survival time

1.4.2.1 Probability density function

  • For continuous case: rate
\[\begin{aligned} f(t)&=\lim_{\delta\rightarrow 0^+}\frac{P(t\le T \le t+\delta)}{\delta}\\ &=\lim_{\delta\rightarrow 0}\frac{S(t)-S(t+\delta)}{\delta}\\ &=-\frac{dS(t)}{dt} \end{aligned}\]

\[ \therefore f(t)dt=-dS(t) \leftrightarrow S'(t)=-f(t) \]

  • For discrete case: \(f(t_j)=P(T=t_j)\)

  • Cumulative distribution function \(F(t)\) \[ F(t)=Pr(T\le t)=\int_0^t f(x)dx=1-S(t) \]

1.4.2.2 위험함수(hazard function)

  • 위험함수는 어떤 환자가 \(t\) 시점까지는 생존했다가 \(t\) 시점 바로 직후에 사망하게 되는 순간위험률을 의미
  • The instantaneous rate of failure at \(T=t\), conditional on survival to time \(t\)
    • Instantaneous failure rate assuming that the individual has survived to time \(t\)
    • Force of mortality 0 Instantaneous conditional probability that an event occurs between (\(t, t+\delta\)) given that the individual has survived up to time \(t\)

\[ \begin{aligned} \lambda(t)&=\lim_{\delta\rightarrow 0^+}\frac{P(t\le T \le t+\delta|T\ge t)}{\delta}\\ &=\lim_{\delta\rightarrow 0^+}\frac{P(t\le T \le t+\delta)}{\delta\cdot P(T\ge t)}\\ &=\frac{f(t)}{S(t)} \\ &=-\frac{-\frac{d}{dt}S(t)}{S(t)}\\ &=-\frac{d}{dt}\log S(t) \end{aligned} \]

  • 누적위험함수(cumulative hazard function)

\[ \Lambda(t)=\int_0^t \lambda(s)ds \]

  • 생존함수와 누적위험함수의 관계

\[ \log S(t)=-\int_0^t \lambda(s)ds = -\Lambda(t) \]

\[ \therefore S(t)=e^{-\Lambda(t)} \]

  • 위험함수의 이해를 위해
    • \(P(A|B)\)는 조건부확률을 뜻하는 것으로 주어진 사건 \(B\)가 일어났다는 가정하에 \(A\)가 일어난 확률을 의미
    • \(P(t<T<t+dt|T \ge t)\)는 어떤 대상이 특정한 시간 \(t\)보다 더 생존하였다고 가정할 때 그 대상이 특정한 시간으로부터 아주 짧은 시간 간격 안에 실패할 확률을 의미
    • 이때, 위험함수는 조건부확률을 단위시간으로 나눈 것으로 확률에서 비율의 개념으로 바뀜
    • 조건부확률은 0과 1사이의 값, 위험함수는 0부터 무한대의 값을 가짐
    • 예를 들어, 조건부확률이 1/3일때 시간 간격이 1/2day라면 확률을 시간 간격으로 나눈 값은 0.67/day가 되고 시간 간격을 1/14week라면 확률을 시간 간격으로 나눈 값은 4.67/week이 됨
  • 위험함수 특징
    • 항상 음수가 아닌 값을 가짐
    • 0부터 무한대의 값을 가짐
  • 위험함수를 고려하는 이유
    • 생존함수는 그 시간까지의 누적생존을 나타내지만, 위험함수는 순간적인 실패가능성을 의미
    • 생존데이터에 맞는 특정 모형을 찾는 데 사용할 수 있음(지수분포, 와이블분포, 로그정규분포 등)
    • 생존 데이터를 수학적으로 분석할 때 매개체로 이용

  • \(\lambda_4(t)\) is an example of a Bathtub Shaped Hazard(Failure) Rate function
    • The hazard rate associated with the risk of death for a human subject is often modeled using
  • \(\lambda_5(t)\) is a Mound Shaped Hazard Rate function

    • Typical pattern of risk after a major surgery

1.4.2.4 Summary

  • 누적분포함수(culmulative distribution function, CDF)

\[ F(t)=P(T \le t)=\int_0^{t}f(x)dx=1-S(t), \,\,\, 0<t<\infty \]

  • 확률밀도함수(probability density function, PDF)

\[ f(t)=\frac{d}{dt}F(t)=-\frac{d}{dt}S(t) \]

  • 생존함수(survival function) \[ S(t)=Pr(T\ge t)=\int_t^{\infty} f(x)dx=1-F(t) \]

  • 위험함수(hazard function)

\[ \lambda(t)=\frac{f(t)}{S(t)} \]

  • 누적위험함수(cumulative hazard function)

\[ \Lambda(t)=\int_{0}^{t}\lambda(u)du \]

  • 생존함수와 누적위험함수

\[ \frac{d}{dt}log(S(t))=-\frac{f(t)}{S(t)}=-\lambda(t) \]

따라서

\[ log(S(t))=-\Lambda(t) \leftarrow \rightarrow S(t)=exp(-\Lambda(t))=exp(-\int_0^t \lambda(u) du) \]

1.4.3 Discrete survival time

  • Assume survival time \(T\): discrete mass points, \(x_1<x_2< \cdots\)

  • Probability mass function \[ f(x_i)=Pr[T=x_i], \,\,\, i=1,2,\cdots \]

1.4.3.1 Survival function

\[ S(t)=Pr(T\ge t)=\sum_{j,x_j\ge t}f(x_j)=\sum_{j}f(x_j)H(x_j-t) \]

      where \(H(x)\) is heaviside function

  • \(H(\cdot)\) is not differentiable at \(x=0\)

  • Define

\[ \frac{dH(x)}{dx}=\lim_{\Delta x\rightarrow 0}\frac{H(x+\Delta x)-H(x)}{\Delta x}=\delta(x) \]

      where \(\delta(0)\) is the Dirac delta function defined as

\[ \delta(x)=\begin{cases} 1, \,\,\, if \,\,\, x=0 \\ 0, \,\,\, if \,\,\, x\ne 0 \end{cases} \]

  • \(dH(x)=\delta(x)dx\)

\[ \int_{-\infty}^t dH(x)=\int_{-\infty}^t\delta(x)dx=H(t)-H(\infty)=H(t) \]

\[ \therefore H(t)=\begin{cases} 1, \,\,\, if \,\,\, t<0 \\ 0, \,\,\, if \,\,\, t\ge 0 \end{cases} \]

  • \(f(t)=-\frac{dS(t)}{dt}\)로 부터

\[ \begin{aligned} f(t)dt &=-dS(t)\\ &=-\sum_{j}f(x_j)dH(x_j-t)\\ &=\sum_{j}f(x_j)\delta(x_j-t) \,\,\, (\therefore dH(x)=\delta(x)dx) \end{aligned} \]

1.4.3.2 Hazard function

  • hazard function

\[ \lambda(t)=\lim_{\Delta t \rightarrow 0^+}\frac{P(t\le T<t+\Delta t|T\ge t)}{\Delta t}=\frac{f(x_j)}{F(t)}=\lambda_j, \,\,\, t=x_j \]

\[ \therefore \lambda(t)=\sum_{j}\lambda_j\delta(t-x_j), \,\,\, \delta(x)=1,\,\,\, if \,\,\, x=0, \,\,\, otherwise \,\,\ 0 \]

  • Hazard at \(x_j\) is defined as the conditional probability of failure at \(x_j\)

\[ \lambda_j = Pr(T=x_j|T\ge x_j)=\frac{f(x_j)}{F(x_j)} , \,\,\, j=1, \cdots \]

  • So we define

\[ \lambda(t) = \frac{f(t)}{F(t)} \]

  • Cumulative hazard function

\[ \Lambda (t)=\sum_{j, x_j\le t} \lambda_j = \sum_{j}\lambda_j H(t-x_j)=\int_0^t \lambda(s)ds \]

\[ \therefore d\Lambda(t)=\sum_{j}\lambda(t)\delta(t-x_j)dt \]

1.4.3.3 Product representation of \(S(t)\)

  • Annals of Statistics, 1990

\[ S(t)=\prod_{j,x_j<t}(1-\lambda_j) \]

  • The survival probability at time \(t\) is obtained by taking the product of the conditional survival probabilities \(1-d\Lambda(u)\) over infinitesimal intervals up to time \(t\)

  • It is easy to show that

\[ \begin{aligned} S(x_j) &= Pr(T\ge x_j)\\ &=Pr(T\ge x_j|T\ge x_{j-1})Pr(T\ge x_{j-1})\\ &=(1-\lambda_{j-1})\cdot S(x_{j-1}), \,\,\, (\cdot \lambda_{j-1}=Pr(T=x_{j-1}|T\ge x_{j-1}))\\ &=(1-\lambda_{j-1})\cdot (1-\lambda_{j-2})\cdot S(x_{j-2})\\ & \,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\, \vdots \\ &= S(t)=\prod_{j,x_j<t}(1-\lambda_j) \end{aligned} \]

1.4.3.4 \(T\) has continuous and discrete parts (mixture of these)

  • \(T_d\): discrete survival time \(\sim S_d\)

\[ x_1 < x_2 < \cdots < x_j: \,\,\, jump \,\,\, point \]

  • \(T_c\): continuous survival time \(\sim S_c\)

  • Assume \(T_d\) and \(T_c\) are independent

  • \(T=min(T_c, T_d)\): death can be determined at first are between \(T_c\) and \(T_d\)

  1. \(S(t)=P(min(T_c,T_d)\ge t)=P(T_c \ge t) P(T_d\ge t)=S_c(t)S_d(t)\)

      Survival function at time \(t\) is the product of two survival tuncions at time \(t\)

  1. \(f(t)dt=-dS(t)=S_c(t)f_c(t)dt+S_c(t)f_d(t)dt\)

  2. \(\lambda(t)dt=-\frac{dS(t)}{S(t)}=\lambda_c(t)dt+\lambda_d(t)dt\)

  3. \(\Lambda(t)=\Lambda_c(t)+\Lambda_d(t)\)
